As the complete model would be enormous, it has been drawn on three levels. First of all, the zoomed out level. In this model, all the players are drawn. They have arrows from themselves towards all players. For example, for player 1, this means that he will never doubt between states where only information of player 4 or 5 is different. Therefore, there is no arrow between 4 and 5 for player 1. However, he is always in doubt about his own information, therefore there are arrows from himself towards all other players. Reflexive arrows are present everywhere, if we know the details, we know we know them. When we zoom in we get to the model for the cards of a particular player (player 1 in this case). Here there are arrows between all states for player 1, but only reflexive arrows for all the other players. As a result, all other players know details about the cards, except for player 1. When we zoom in to the smallest level, a part of the kripke model for all possibilities for one card from player 1 are drawn. Note that this is only a subset of all the possibilities for this one card. For each card, all the players have reflexive arrows, but the only arrows between cards are from player 1. This means that only player 1 is in doubt about his own cards.
